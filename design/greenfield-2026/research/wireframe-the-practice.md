# The Practice

**File:** `the-practice.html` **Nav label:** "The Practice"
**Purpose (one line):** Show a sophisticated prospect exactly how a Driftwood engagement runs, and prove that coordination is a measured discipline with a price, so believing the thesis converts into requesting the Review.

**Structural header engraving:** `driftwood_steady_through_noise`, one full-bleed plate inside the framed `.inner`, directly above the eyebrow, at Horizon scale. It is the page's only engraving. Why it wins: hierarchy #1 (differentiation) is this header's job, and "two steady navigators holding a line through noise" is the attending promise made visible (one party stays accountable for the whole household as complexity compounds). Meeting-of-waters and headland illustrate the noun "coordination"; this page's single idea is the *practice*, the standing discipline that carries a household year over year, so the navigators plate wins and the confluence images stay in reserve for the Tax Atlas and Record surfaces.

## Section-by-section

The four-part argument carries roman numerals (I to IV) so the spine is trackable end to end with no gap. The header, the band, and the three closing beats (founder, specimen, door) are the coda and framing, deliberately un-numbered, so a reader never hits a missing marker mid-argument.

- **Content:** Engraving plate. Eyebrow `THE PRACTICE`. H1 (Satoshi, tight, ~20ch): "Most plans are written once and filed. A coordination engagement never stops running." Lede (Erode): "This is how the work actually runs: who does what, in what order, and what recurs every year. The deliverable is not a binder you shelve. It is a standing discipline that keeps every decision in your financial life talking to the others, so the next decision starts where the last one ended." No CTA; the page earns the door first.
- **Absorbs:** `howitworks.html` hero framing.

### 3. I. The operating model it borrowed
- **One idea:** High-stakes fields run on a coordinating function with one accountable party; wealth kept the specialists and dropped that seat.
- **Content:** Section head (brass `.sn`): "I. Wealth borrowed the specialists. It forgot the attending." One moving paragraph of the tumor-board frame plus a three-up analog strip (Medicine / Aviation / Wealth), using the built `.why/.analog` top-ruled cards. Copy: "A cancer diagnosis is not treated by one brilliant doctor. It is decided by a room: surgeons, oncologists, radiologists, and pathologists review the same patient together, and one person stays accountable for the whole patient, not a single organ. That is the attending physician." Close on the canonical turn, kept **only here**: "Your CPA, your attorney, your insurance agent, your banker: each is a gifted specialist. In most financial lives, almost no one is the attending. Driftwood is." A single serif pull-quote on a brass left rule carries the turn. No numbers yet.
- **Absorbs:** `coordination.html` sections I to II (tumor-board / attending, aviation cross-check).

### 4. II. The proof: one household, one variable
- **One idea:** Coordination is a discipline with a measurable price, and the price is the value that appears *only* when the four seats move in order.
- **Content:** Open with the illustrative frame **up front, not footnoted** (behavioral, hierarchy #2 trust): "Here is one worked illustration, not a client." Section head: "II. Same family. Same eight million dollars. One variable." Intro introduces the Harris family once: roughly $8,000,000 across a taxable brokerage, RSUs, pre-tax and Roth retirement, an HSA, two properties, a closely held business, and a $2,000,000 life policy, spread across a half-dozen specialists. Then draw a state line.
  - **Re-attribute the number to coordination, not a lone lever (CMO, hierarchy #1 differentiation).** Show the counterfactual explicitly: each seat acting alone leaves value on the table. The attorney drafts an ILIT, the CPA's conversion ladder collides with it, the insurance seat funds a policy that inflates the taxed estate because no one sequenced the moves. The figure the page keeps is what surfaces only when the four seats act in order.
  - Two-column comparison, **teal on figures only, rounded** (behavioral): Illinois, the estate-tax trap (4.95% flat, $4,000,000 exemption, not portable, not indexed): uncoordinated exposure roughly $680,000, due nine months after death with no buyer for the illiquid pieces. Sequenced across all four seats (policy into an ILIT so the benefit leaves the estate, discounted business interests gifted in the right order, low-basis assets held for the step-up): roughly $285,000. The coordination delta is about $395,000. Texas: no state income or estate tax, so the estate problem vanishes at the border and the coordination value moves to a lifetime of Roth conversions at a 0% state rate the family would otherwise miss. Lever-contrast row (Illinois: sequenced ILIT + gifting | Texas: conversion ladder at a 0% state rate).
  - Close on the **number alone** (editorial): "Same family. Same eight million. In Illinois the sequenced result is worth about $395,000; in Texas the same family's biggest opportunity is a lifetime of tax-free growth." Do not repeat the "attending" line here.
  - Quiet inline Atlas link, softened so it does not read as a calculator that produced these figures (CMO + behavioral): "Your own state draws a different line. The Tax Atlas shows where."
  - Source line: "The Harris family, illustrative. Figures are educational models to be confirmed with your tax and legal advisors under current law."
- **Absorbs:** `coordination.html` sections III to IV (Harris balance sheet, Illinois vs Texas). The interactive dependency "lever" map is **not** rebuilt here; it survives as a static excerpt or a quiet link into coordination content on The Record.

### 5. III. How the first year runs
- **One idea:** The first year is a defined sequence carried by three working sessions, each modeled before anything is bought or sold.
- **Content:** **The timeline is now a single object (IA / UX / editorial, hierarchy #3).** The old phase-overview and the old three-conversations section are merged: the meetings *are* the phases, shown once. Section head: "III. How the first year runs, month zero through implementation." Three cards (soft #f7f5f0, 1px line, square), each a phase and a meeting at once:
  - **Meeting One, ~90 min, The Intake and Intent (first ~2 weeks).** "Nothing is bought or sold. We map your whole system: every account, the last return, the estate documents, your cash-flow needs, and the professionals already in your life. And we capture what paperwork never does: what your family is trying to accomplish, what must never happen, and the language you use for your own life. It becomes the yardstick every later decision is measured against." (Salvaged verbatim from the cut meetings section per editorial.) *What you get:* a written picture of your household as one system, where it leaks, and what is uncoordinated.
  - **Meeting Two, ~90 min, The Architecture (first ~60 days).** "We walk your household as one system and produce the written plan: where value leaks, where the seams are, how one decision moves through all four seats before it is made, with the specific hand-offs to your CPA and attorney in plain language." (Meeting name is a design-lead label; confirm final wording with founder.)
  - **Meeting Three, ~2 hours, The First Operating Session (next ~30 to 60 days).** "The hand-offs execute in sequence, each modeled before it is made. We walk your Wealth Operating Manual end to end: confirm the decisions that still hold, retire the ones that do not, open the decisions ahead (a sale, a move, a conversion ladder), and set the exact conditions that would reopen each. Every year after runs the same way." That last line is the hinge into Section 6.
  - Each card ends with a short serif "what it asks of you" line. No em dashes (ranges written "~30 to 60 days").
- **Absorbs:** `howitworks.html` five-phase timeline **and** three-meeting section, merged into one surface. **This is the collapse the IA, UX, and editorial critiques demanded:** the standalone phase-overview section is cut, the tease-then-expand of Phase IV is gone, and the reader walks the sequence once.

### 6. IV. What recurs, every year
- **One idea:** After year one the relationship is a governed annual rhythm: quarterly reviews, event-triggered modeling, and one Annual Wealth Operating Review.
- **Content:** Section head: "IV. What recurs, every year." Intro: "Every institution runs on a cadence. Medicine has the annual physical. A company has its board meeting. A family office has its investment committee. Driftwood has the Annual Wealth Operating Review, and its job is not a performance update. It is to make sure every decision ahead is a better decision." Cadence strip (three top-ruled cells): **Quarterly**, the Operating Review (new decisions, an updated map, loss harvesting and remaining bracket room shared with your CPA, not left to April). **At every event** (a liquidity event, a vest, a gift, a move to a new state, modeled before the decision is made). **Annually**, the Wealth Operating Review (emphasized cell: the four seats sit down together, the record judged, intent re-examined, every open decision reopened). Compact six-domain grid (Portfolio, Taxes, Estate, Insurance, Cash flow, Business), each a short bulleted "what gets reviewed." Quiet sideways link: "The full annual agenda and its deliverables live in The Record."
- **Absorbs:** `review.html` in full (cadence strip, six domains, the AWOR as the governing event). `review.html` retires; the AWOR deliverable detail migrates to The Record.

## What the critiques changed
- **Cut the tripled timeline (IA, UX Lead, Editorial), decided by hierarchy #3 narrative clarity over #4 conversion.** The standalone phase-overview and the three-conversations section are merged into one Section 5 where the meetings are the phases; Phase IV opens once in Section 6. The reader walks month-zero-to-forever a single time, and the "one standing discipline" claim stops being blurred by three retellings.
- **Re-attributed the $395,000 to coordination, not a lone estate lever (Wealth CMO), decided by hierarchy #1 differentiation over #4 conversion.** Section 4 now shows the counterfactual (each seat acting alone leaves it on the table) so the number is the uncopyable coordination delta, not an ILIT any attorney could name. The figure stays loud; only its attribution changed.
- **Led Section 4 with the illustrative frame and rounded the figures (Behavioral Economist), decided by hierarchy #2 trust over #4 conversion.** "One worked illustration, not a client" moves up front, dollars round to "about $395,000 / roughly $680,000," and "the same engine that produced these figures" is cut so a fabricated household is never dressed as live tool output.
- **Differentiated the two asks so the tool feeds the door (Conversion Strategist), decided by hierarchy #3 over #4.** The Atlas keeps its single earned link but now owns the "begin on your own numbers in two minutes" promise exclusively; the Section 9 door is re-scoped to "sit down with the attending," and the redundant self-serve row is cut, so the Atlas answers "is there anything here for me" and the Review answers "who stays accountable," sequential rather than rival.
- **Two smaller fixes:** the repeated "attending" canonical line is kept in Section 3 and Section 4 ends on the number alone (Editorial); the navy band is re-pointed to a distinct anticipatory idea rather than restating the hero (IA), an override justified because the band's tie to the homepage map earns its keep once its idea no longer duplicates Section 1.
