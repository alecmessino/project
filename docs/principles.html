First Principles

What Driftwood believes.

No services, no fees — just the ideas the firm is built on. If you're deciding whether to introduce us to someone, read this first.

The Constitution

Financial decisions should not surprise one another.

Our observation

Complex personal wealth is an operating problem before it is an investment problem.

What we know
  • Important financial decisions interact.
  • Complexity grows through relationships, not assets.
  • Different professionals operate on different timelines.
  • Great expertise without coordination produces inconsistent outcomes.
Therefore, we
  • Map the system before building the portfolio.
  • Coordinate professionals instead of replacing them.
  • Build one operating framework for the household.
  • Measure success by how well decisions reinforce one another.
The product

Confidence that every important financial decision has already considered the others.
