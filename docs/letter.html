Latest · Nº 01 · 2026

Every portfolio has two returns.

There is the return your statement shows, and the return you actually keep after tax. For most households the gap between them is not decided by which funds they own; it is decided by where those funds are held, the order gains are realized, and whether the estate and the portfolio were designed in view of each other. A good year, uncoordinated, quietly leaks. The same year, coordinated, compounds.

This first letter walks one illustrative household through that gap, and the three places coordination tends to fail: asset location, the sequence of realized gains, and an estate plan that has never met the investment plan.
