# Driftwood Category Audit — v1

*A standing editorial operating manual, not a one-off review.*
First run: 2026-07-13 · 15 pages scored · Method: one independent reviewer per page against the rubric below.

---

## 0 · How to use this document

This is the editorial constitution for every public page. Two jobs:

1. **A standing rubric** — the definitions, the ban list, the guardrail, and the target hierarchy that
   every page (new or edited) is held to. These are the sections that *don't* expire.
2. **A dated scorecard** — a snapshot of where each page stands today, with one ranked improvement each.
   Re-run it after any batch of page edits and bump the version (v1 → v2 …); keep the old scorecard for
   the trend line.

**To re-run:** give each major page to an independent reviewer (human or agent) with §1 as the brief —
score 1–5 on the four dimensions with a one-line justification grounded in a specific line, flag legacy
language against §1.5, and answer the guardrail question (§1.3). Score pages independently (don't let one
page's read anchor the next), then synthesize. The rubric in §1 is the source of truth; any harness only
applies it.

## 1 · Standing principles (these do not expire)

**1.1 The category.** Driftwood's product is **coordination** — running a household's financial life as
one system. It is *not* investment outperformance. Every dollar figure on every page is **evidence that
coordination matters, never the promise.**

**1.2 The identity.** The message has shifted, and we lean into the newer one:

> ~~Better after-tax outcomes.~~ → **Better coordinated decision systems.**

**1.3 The guardrail test.** Every page must pass:

> *If I removed the dollar figure entirely, would this page still convince someone that coordination
> matters?*

If yes, the category is built correctly on that page. If no, the page is selling a number, not the
product — fix it before shipping.

**1.4 The target Atlas hierarchy.** The order of a data page is deliberate — **meaning before dollars:**

```
Planning Dynamics        ·  why this environment changes the coordination equation   (meaning)
        ↓
Coordination Opportunity ·  the illustrative impact                                   (evidence)
        ↓
Methodology              ·  how the estimate was produced                             (proof)
```

Dollars never sit first. The eye should land on the *why* before the *how much*.

**1.5 The legacy-language ban list.** These terms belong to the old world (better manager, market-beating)
and must not appear as claims: **alpha · outperform · "better returns" · "investment advantage" ·
"tax alpha" · beat.** One exception, applied with judgment: a **deliberate reframe** that *rejects* the
frame ("not which state is *better*"; "they don't *outperform* because they buy better investments") is
allowed — it uses the word to disarm it. A named, disclaimed engine concept ("Structural Alpha — *not* a
market forecast") is allowed. Anything that reads as a genuine outperformance promise is not.

**1.6 The rubric.** Score each 1–5 (5 = excellent):

| Dimension | The question |
|---|---|
| **Recognition** | Does a visitor immediately recognize *their own* situation on this page? |
| **Category** | Does the page reinforce Driftwood as coordination architecture (a system), not a better manager? |
| **Journey** | Is there a clear, single, well-placed next step onward? |
| **Institutional credibility** | Would a CPA, estate attorney, or family-office pro feel this *elevates* — not replaces — their work? |

## 3 · What the scorecard says

**3.1 Category coherence is the asset — protect it (4.8/5).** Every one of the fifteen pages scores 4 or
5 on Category. A homepage, an Atlas, a Comparison engine, a Household Record, a fee page, and an
investment thesis all speak the same language: *coordination is the system; the portfolio is one module
inside it.* This is the coherence that is far harder to build than another feature, and the standing rule
(§6) exists to defend it.

**3.2 The guardrail holds everywhere (15/15).** Strip the dollars and every page still argues that
coordination matters — the checklists, the estate cliffs, the operating-system diagrams, and the "nothing
important happens in isolation" creed all carry the case on their own. The dollar figure is doing exactly
the job it should: evidence, not promise. *One narrow pass:* the **Atlas index** — remove its
"Coordination Opportunity" column and the table risks reducing to a commodity "top LT rate" reference; its
coordination framing lives mostly in the CTA, not the grid.

**3.3 The two systemic gaps are Recognition (3.7) and Journey (3.6).** These are not page-specific bugs;
they are the same two patterns recurring across the site:

- **Recognition — pages open on the method before the mirror.** Seven pages (Atlas index, Comparison,
  Operating System, Philosophy, How We Invest, Tax Diagnostic, After-Tax Review) lead with a principle
  ("Evidence over prediction," "The return you keep is not the return you earn," "You build a system")
  before the visitor sees *their own life* on the page. The fix is nearly always the same shape: **one
  situational line near the top** that names the reader's circumstance (the mover, the concentrated-
  position holder, the two-generation household with a live domicile question) before the argument
  begins. The pages that score 5 — Homepage and Fees — already do this ("For households whose financial
  lives have outgrown disconnected advice"; "$6M single brokerage account and a $2.5M business owner with
  entities, a trust, and multi-state taxes").

- **Journey — competing co-equal CTAs dilute the single next step.** The recurring failure is *two or
  three same-weight "primary" buttons plus an onward-link row*, so no single action dominates. Worst case
  is **Philosophy (2)** — three co-equal "read on" links and no primary. The **Homepage (3)** has the
  same disease: three same-weight teal primaries point to three destinations while the terminal meeting
  CTA appears once, at the very bottom. The fix: **one dominant primary per page**, everything else
  demoted to ghost/secondary.

**3.4 Institutional credibility is strong (4.6) with one soft spot.** Most pages explicitly hand work to
the named professional ("· with your estate attorney," "You keep the CPA and attorney you trust," "No one
is replaced; everyone works from one plan"). The exception is **How We Invest (3)** — it discusses tax and
estate as things Driftwood *does* and never names the client's CPA or attorney as partners it coordinates
*with*, so it risks reading as replace rather than elevate. One sentence fixes it.

## 4 · Legacy-language register

The coordination voice is **clean.** Across fifteen pages there is not a single naive outperformance or
market-beating claim in visible copy. Every flag below is either a deliberate reframe (allowed) or a small
methodology/provenance phrase. Two are worth tightening.

| Page | Term | In context | Judgment | Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tax Diagnostic | **tax alpha** | Visible source footnote renders the script filename `scripts/tax_alpha.py` | **reframe** | Cite the method under a neutral display name (e.g. "Driftwood After-Tax model · 30y proxy-spliced cache") so the banned term never renders. Keep the real filename in code. |
| After-Tax Review · Homepage | **after-tax edge** | "modeled as an illustrative +3.7–4.7%/yr after-tax edge" | **reframe** | "edge" leans old-world; prefer "after-tax **retention**" / "after-tax value **kept**." |
| Comparison | *better* | "Not which state is **better** — the wrong question." | keep | Rejects the frame; leave. |
| Family Office | *advantage* / *outperform* | "The structure is the advantage"; meta-description "don't outperform because they buy better investments" | keep | Deliberate reframe onto structure; "outperform" is meta-only (not on-page). Leave. |
| How We Invest | **Structural Alpha** | "…is what we call Structural Alpha. It is not a market forecast" | keep | Named, disclaimed engine concept. Leave. |
| Tax Diagnostic | out-perform / out-earn | Inside disclaimers that *reject* the frame ("NOT claimed to out-earn pre-tax") | keep | Reinforces the category. Leave. |
| Crossing Brief | *advantage* | "a major basis advantage" (community-property step-up) | keep | Describes a tax-law feature, not investment outperformance. Leave. |

**Net:** two tightening edits (both "reframe"), zero removals. The category discipline is real.

## 6 · Standing rule

**No new features until this backlog is worked down.** As of v1, the platform's homepage, Atlas, Comparison
engine, Crossing Brief, Household Record, and navigation all speak one language — a coherence that is
rarer and more valuable than any additional feature. Protect it before expanding.

Work order, highest leverage first:
1. **Two legacy-language reframes** (§4) — objective, minutes each.
2. **Journey consolidation** — one dominant primary per page (Philosophy, Homepage, Atlas state, Family
   Office, Our Story). A repeatable pattern, not bespoke copy.
3. **Recognition openers** — one situational line per page (the seven pages at Recognition 3). The single
   highest-impact theme; do these deliberately, one voice.
4. **How We Invest** — the one institutional-credibility sentence.
5. **Atlas hierarchy** — evolve the state page toward *Planning Dynamics → Coordination Opportunity →
   Methodology* (§1.4) once the copy work above is settled.

Each item above traces to a specific page and dimension in §5. Close them, then run **v2**.
