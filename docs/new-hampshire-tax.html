How New Hampshire taxes investors

Every state taxes investors differently. Here is how New Hampshire treats capital gains at the top rate, the marriage penalty, estate and inheritance tax at death, municipal-bond interest, the §1202 QSBS exclusion, and a harvested loss — a plain reference to the state's tax code.

New Hampshire levies no state income tax on capital gains; no state death tax; common-law step-up (only half of jointly-held property).

New Hampshire taxes neither wages nor capital gains, and its former Interest & Dividends tax was fully repealed effective 2025 — so investment income is now untaxed at the state level. There is no general sales tax either.
