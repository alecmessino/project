Frequently asked — 7 on Ohio
How are capital gains taxed in Ohio?

Loss treatment conforms to federal: capital losses net against gains and carry forward. Top effective long-term rate 3.13%. Quirk: school districts can add ~0.25–2%.

Does Ohio have a state estate or inheritance tax?

No state estate or inheritance tax — only the federal estate tax applies.

Is there a marriage penalty in Ohio?

One bracket schedule applies to both single and joint filers — a structural marriage penalty for two earners.

How does Ohio tax municipal-bond interest?

Only in-state municipal-bond interest escapes state tax; bonds from other states are taxed. The classic in-state muni preference that rewards a home-state ladder.

Does Ohio follow the federal QSBS (§1202) exclusion?

Conforms to IRC §1202 — the federal qualified small business stock gain exclusion carries through to the state return.

What happens to a capital loss you carry forward in Ohio?

Capital losses carry forward under the federal Section 1212 rules — a harvested loss nets against gains and rolls forward until used.
