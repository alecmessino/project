# The Record

**Purpose:** One canonical proof-tier surface that introduces the Harris household exactly once, makes the consolidation visible as its own map, and shows a coordinated household's operating paper as dated specimens that resolve to a single door. Nine off-nav pages (~11,000 words) become three living surfaces: this index, `register.html`, and `awor.html`.

**Structural header engraving:** `driftwood_confluence_reach`, Section 1 masthead only, ink-on-limestone, square, hairline rule beneath. The Record is an archive: dated decisions converging and receding over years, so vanishing-perspective depth reads as accumulation and legacy. It beats `confluence_headland` (too directional, over-signals navigation) and `meeting_of_waters` (that figure belongs to Coordination / The Practice). One engraving, spent here, honoring the one-per-inner-page ceiling. Unchanged from the draft; both the UX Lead and the brief affirm it.

## Section-by-section

### 1. Record Masthead
- **One idea:** This is the real operating paper, shown plainly.
- **Content:** Engraving band across the top, hairline rule, then the title. Eyebrow (Satoshi small-caps): THE RECORD. Display title (Erode): "The paper a coordinated household actually runs on." Standfirst (Erode, generous measure): "Coordination is not a posture. It leaves documents: dated, reasoned, connected to each other. What follows is one household's operating record, reproduced as its own pages produce it. A composite household, the figures illustrative." No button, no CTA. **The small-caps "ILLUSTRATIVE SAMPLE / FICTIONAL HOUSEHOLD / FIGURES COMPOSED" caption is cut.** One plain disclaimer inside the prose, said once, reads as confidence; the redundant caption only adds flinch and buys no disclosure the prose lacks (the Section 2 composite line carries the rest).
- **Absorbs:** the scattered "what the Record is" framing from `record.html`.

### 2. The Harris Household (single introduction)
- **One idea:** Meet the single household whose record you are about to read.
- **Content:** Dossier cover card, folio-labeled CASE 001. Erode profile: "The Harris household. Two principals in their late fifties, one operating company they still run, roughly eight million dollars in investable assets, residency and revenue split across Illinois and Texas, three adult children, and a charitable intention they have talked about for a decade but never structured." Beside it a Satoshi attribute table (Composition, Business interest, Investable assets $8M, Residency IL / TX, Next generation, Open questions), teal on the $8M figure only. Composite line: "A composite. No such family exists. The tensions are real because they recur." Every later folio says "the Harrises" with no re-introduction.
- **Absorbs:** the five separate Harris retellings across the nine legacy pages, collapsed to this one canonical caption.

### 3. Contents of the Record (the working spine)
- **One idea:** The record is one living list, and every standing document is a dated view of it.
- **Content:** This section now carries the load-bearing claim the draft buried at Section 7. State the spine first, in Erode: "There is one living list. The Constitution, the allocation policy, the memos, the transition plan, the decision log are not separate documents so much as dated views onto it." Beneath it, a folio-style index in two quiet columns with **anchor-jump links** (the map is active, not passive text). Left, THE LIVING LIST AND ITS VIEWS: 01 Opportunity Register (the spine), 02 Constitution ("rules set once"), 03 Capital Allocation Policy, 04 Investment Committee Memo, 05 Transition Plan, 06 Operating Manual, 07 Decision Register ("decisions logged over time"), each a one-line gloss. Right, OPEN IN FULL: Opportunity Register → `register.html`, Annual Wealth Operating Review → `awor.html`. **The two "open the full..." lateral links live here and only here**, as wayfinding, so they never appear as escape hatches at peak intent later. Folio numerals in Satoshi mono small-caps, editorial blue on the numerals only. **The draft's closing italic ("...telling one household's story five times over") is cut**: it narrates the edit to a reader who never saw the five old pages.
- **Absorbs:** gives the archive the navigation the nine off-nav pages never had; fuses the map and the contents into a single pass rather than listing the page's contents twice.

### 4. The Opportunity Register (the spine, living specimen)
- **One idea:** One living list is the thing every standing document is a view of.
- **Content:** Promoted to lead the specimens, immediately after Harris. The richest table on the page, mirroring the homepage exhibit: columns Opportunity / Est. value / Status / Owner, six to eight rows, teal on the value column only (e.g. "Charitable remainder structure for the concentrated position | $220,000 | Modeling | Alec + estate counsel"). Framing line (Erode): "This is the list. Everything that follows is a view of it." Because the differentiated dollar appears in the very first specimen, the reader meets consequence, not apparatus. Ends on provenance-by-implication (dated rows, "current between reviews" marker); **no closing provenance sentence, no exit link.**
- **Absorbs:** `opportunity-register.html` as its canonical home (that page already called the others "scoped views"). Full list opens at `register.html` via the Contents link only.

### 5. The Standing Views (two archetype folios)
- **One idea:** The standing rules are dated slices of the same register: one set once, one accruing over time.
- **Content:** **Thinned from six consecutive process folios to two archetypes**, so the reader is not walked through a governance binder before reaching consequence. FOLIO: Constitution ("rules set once"): one or two numbered articles verbatim, e.g. "Article 7. Tax is a coordination problem before it is an investment problem," shown as what an article prevents, not just what it says. FOLIO: Decision Register ("decisions logged over time"): four dated rows, columns Date / Decision / Rationale / Reversible?, sample "2025-03-14 | Deferred Roth conversion to Q4 | State residency for the year still unsettled; converting now risks the Illinois rate on the full amount | Yes, until Dec 31." Square corners, hairline rules, Satoshi headers, Erode rationale. The other four views (Capital Allocation Policy, IC Memo, Transition Plan, Operating Manual) are **named and glossed in Section 3, not excerpted inline**: deep reading becomes optional depth, not a mandatory gauntlet. No provenance sentence, no exit link.
- **Absorbs:** `constitution.html` and `decision-register.html` as the two shown archetypes; `capital-allocation.html`, `ic-memo.html`, `transition-plan.html`, `manual.html` all die as standalone pages and are absorbed into this page, referenced in the Contents index.

### 6. The Annual Wealth Operating Review (flagship specimen)
- **One idea:** Once a year the entire record is worked, end to end, into one reviewed document.
- **Content:** Kept last before the door; it earns the crescendo. The Harris Illinois-versus-Texas question carried through: "On roughly eight million dollars and one business sale, the difference between an Illinois-domiciled year and a Texas-domiciled one came to about $395,000 in state tax. The Review does not assert an answer. It documents the defensible split, the days-count evidence, and the second-order effects on the charitable timing." Shows it pulling the register items and the Constitution article into one yearly working. Teal on the worked figures. Most vertical space of any specimen. **The "Open the full Annual Review" link is removed from here** (it lives in Contents), so the strongest proof on the site flows uninterrupted into the door rather than leaking sideways at maximum intent. Tax Atlas appears as **one quiet in-text footnote**, not a competing link: "the same $395,000 can be explored, state by state, in the Tax Atlas." No closing provenance sentence.
- **Absorbs:** `awor.html`, which survives as the full specimen surface; excerpted here, opened in full via the Contents link.

### 7. Provenance and the Single Door
- **One idea:** The record is the byproduct of one process, and here is where yours begins.
- **Content:** Navy stance band echoing the homepage. Erode: "None of these pages was written for a website. Each is an output of the same process, maintained between reviews and re-worked once a year. **This is what a Coordination Review produces.** You have just read a household's operating record. The only thing it required was the decision to coordinate." **The canonical provenance line is spent exactly once, here**, where it becomes the earned thesis rather than a five-times-repeated stamp. Beneath it, the single understated forward exit: "Request a Coordination Review" → `coordination-review.html`. No second engraving, no founder photo, no urgency.

## What the critiques changed
- **Register promoted to lead the specimens; six-folio parade cut to two archetypes** (Information Architect + Wealth CMO + UX Lead). Differentiation (#1) wins: the single-register-as-spine IS the differentiated claim, so it frames everything and arrives before apparatus. I override the CMO's "lead with AWOR" literally, because the spine is the more differentiating organizing frame; the concession is that the register's own teal value column puts dollars in the very first specimen, and AWOR keeps its earned crescendo.
- **Provenance line spent once, at the door, not after every folio** (Editorial Director + Behavioral Economist + CMO). Narrative clarity (#3) outranks conversion (#4): one earned claim converts better than five repetitions that dull it into a slogan.
- **Section 1 over-disclaiming cut to one plain line; Section 3 "five times over" narration cut** (Behavioral Economist + Editorial Director). Institutional trust (#2): one honest admission reads as confidence; triple disclaiming reads as a firm with thin evidence.
- **Lateral "open the full..." links relocated to Contents only; specimens end clean into the door; Tax Atlas demoted to footnote** (Conversion Strategist + UX Lead). Conversion (#4) resolved without cost to trust or differentiation: proof now sits adjacent to the exit instead of opening onto navigation at peak intent.
