The family-office model

One household, coordinated.

Most families' wealth is run in pieces — the portfolio in one place, the tax return in another, the estate plan in a third. We work the way a family office does: the seat at the table that keeps every piece coordinated, and methodical, as one after-tax plan — across your CPA, your estate attorney, and the decisions you make in your business.

The cadence

Coordination isn't a once-a-year meeting. It's a rhythm — some of it continuous, some of it triggered by what happens in your life.

Continuously
Allocation, location & cash
The portfolio is placed and rebalanced as it drifts; cash is kept where it belongs so nothing is sold at the wrong time.
Quarterly
Harvest & bracket checks
Loss harvesting, drift, and how much room is left in your bracket — reviewed with your CPA, not left to April.
At every event
Before it's taxed
A liquidity event, an equity vest, a gift, a move to a new state — modeled for its after-tax outcome before the decision is made.
Year-end
The coordinated tax plan
Harvesting, Roth conversions, gains realization, and charitable gifts — sequenced together and reconciled to the return you file.

None of these pieces are features. They're the things that decide what you keep — and they only work when someone is coordinating them as one plan. That's the seat Driftwood holds.
