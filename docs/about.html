Our story

Why we built Driftwood.

The largest investors design what they keep after taxes. Most individual portfolios are still built around pre-tax returns. Driftwood exists to close that gap.

Why Driftwood exists

I spent six years at Dimensional Fund Advisors, working alongside some of the country's largest advisory firms — helping them build extraordinary portfolios.

During that time, I helped lead a campaign that found portfolios quietly losing money to tax-inefficient funds. Advisors moved more than $400 million once they saw the after-tax cost.

Then something stopped making sense. Institutions received this level of attention as a matter of course. Families almost never did — though the tools already existed, the discipline already existed, and the architecture already existed. It simply wasn't reaching the people who needed it most.

Driftwood was built to change that.

What we believe

Better investing rarely comes from predicting markets. It comes from making better decisions, consistently, over decades.

So we design around after-tax outcomes, treat taxes as an investment decision rather than an accounting one, and coordinate with each client's CPA and attorney rather than around them.

Above all, not every investment belongs in every account. What matters is where each is held. Great portfolios are engineered, not assembled.

Our principles are set out in full in The Driftwood Principles.

Meet the founder
Alec Messino
Founder

Alec Messino is the founder of Driftwood and a fee-only fiduciary dedicated to helping individuals and families make more informed long-term investment decisions.

Prior to founding Driftwood, he spent six years at Dimensional Fund Advisors supporting many of the country's leading independent advisory firms across portfolio construction, investment research, asset allocation, practice management, and tax-efficient implementation. Earlier in his career, he worked in Asset & Wealth Management at J.P. Morgan, gaining exposure to the planning and portfolio design challenges facing ultra-high-net-worth families.

Today, Alec works closely with clients and their trusted advisors to build evidence-based portfolios designed around what ultimately matters most: the wealth clients keep after taxes.

A CFA Level III candidate, Alec holds the Series 7, Series 66, and SIE securities registrations.
Our philosophy

Markets are uncertain. Taxes are not.

Predictions come and go. Good portfolio architecture compounds.

The goal is not to build the most exciting portfolio. It is to build one you are still grateful to own twenty years from now.
