# Western Springs Corridor — Center-of-Influence (COI) Signal Dossier

**Purpose.** A referral-network map for Driftwood business development while operating out of
Western Springs, IL. It catalogs **professional-services firms** — estate-planning attorneys,
CPAs, and business-law / M&A boutiques — in Western Springs and the adjacent wealth corridor
(Hinsdale, La Grange, Oak Brook / Oakbrook Terrace, and the broader DuPage–west-Cook border)
that serve business owners and high-net-worth families. These are the *centers of influence*
an advisor builds referral relationships with.

**As of:** July 2026 · compiled from public sources · **v1.0**

---

## Scope & method (read this first)

This dossier is deliberately built at the **firm level**, from **public, self-published
marketing** (firm websites and public attorney/CPA directories). Every entry is attributed to a
source below, and details flagged *"per directory/search — verify"* were surfaced in a search
snippet but not confirmed on the firm's own page.

**What this dossier intentionally does _not_ do**, and why:

- **No profiling of named private individuals or families by wealth or "liquidity events."**
  Aggregating a roster of newly-liquid private people to target them is surveillance-style
  profiling, not COI development — and for a FINRA-registered representative (Park Avenue
  Securities / Guardian) it invites privacy and communications-compliance exposure. The
  effective, above-board move is to build relationships with the *advisors* (the CPA, the
  estate attorney, the deal lawyer), who then refer — not to cold-target the sellers.
- **No terms-of-service-violating scraping.** No automated Google News / Google Places
  scraping was written or run. A compliant, repeatable research method is documented at the end
  instead.

> **Before any outreach:** re-verify each firm's current office, personnel, and practice focus
> directly. Inclusion here is *not* vetting, endorsement, or a claim of any existing
> relationship — it is public marketing information assembled for prospecting.

## 5 · Compliant, repeatable research method

To refresh this dossier without ToS-violating scraping or private-individual profiling:

1. **Directory sweep (manual or official API).** Query public attorney/CPA directories
   (Justia, Super Lawyers, Avvo, state CPA society, ISBA) by practice area + town. If
   automating, use each provider's **official API and terms**, not HTML scraping.
2. **Firm-site confirmation.** For each candidate, open the firm's own *People/Attorneys* and
   *Practice Areas* pages to confirm named partners, office address, and focus. Only record
   what the firm publishes about itself.
3. **Recognition corroboration.** Cross-check names against published Super Lawyers / Best
   Lawyers listings (as *recognition*, cited to the listing).
4. **Public deal awareness (optional, deal-level only).** For market awareness, read
   publicly-reported M&A/business news at the **company/deal** level (e.g., Crain's Chicago
   Business, local business journals). Use it to learn *which advisory firms are active* — not
   to build a roster of the individual sellers.
5. **Relationship log, not a wealth dossier.** Track firms and your outreach status. Do not
   compile private individuals' financial details.

**Compliance reminders (fee-only / FINRA-registered context):** keep prospecting communications
within Park Avenue Securities / Guardian advertising-review and recordkeeping policy; respect
Reg S-P and general privacy expectations; treat everything above as public professional
information about *firms*, gathered for legitimate B2B referral development.
