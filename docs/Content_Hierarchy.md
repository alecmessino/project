# Driftwood Content Hierarchy

*One purpose per page. One takeaway per page. One number per page. This document decides what
each page is allowed to say — and, more importantly, what it is not. It exists to prevent the
overlap that editorial iteration naturally creates.*

Companions: `Editorial_Style_Guide.md` (how we write) · `Evidence_Register.md` (which numbers
exist and why). When a draft and this hierarchy disagree, fix the draft — or deliberately amend
the hierarchy first.

**The brand hierarchy, top down — never the reverse:**
Driftwood → institutional portfolio architecture → evidence-based investing → after-tax wealth
management → the resources → the implementations (Structural Alpha, Core Alpha, Tax Lab,
diagnostics, research). Visitors should remember *Driftwood*, not a product name.

---

## Homepage (`index.html`)

- **Purpose:** Introduce the worldview. Make a stranger feel understood about their money in ten seconds.
- **One takeaway:** Portfolios should be built to maximize what you keep, not what they earn — and that's how institutions already invest.
- **Primary CTA:** Tax Lab prospect view ("See your after-tax number").
- **Supporting ideas:** The mission sentence (attention, not intelligence); who it's for; the two-strategies-one-portfolio structure; the honest inversion (less pre-tax, more after tax).
- **Statistics allowed:** +3.7–4.7%/yr (the page's ONE number) · $90,000 → $410,000 · the 9.1%-vs-9.4% inversion sentence.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Sharpe ratios · drawdowns · any research metric outside the clearly-labeled appendix · the ledger return anywhere near the keep-rate dollars (the two-41s collision).
- **Tone:** Calm, declarative, belief-first. Belief → explanation → positioning → proof.
- **Words to avoid:** architecture (spent elsewhere) · engine · optimization · platform · any product name above the pillar notes.
- **Related pages:** Everything — this is the front door. Nothing on it should need explaining twice.
- **Canonical quote:** *"The largest investors in the world don't manage portfolios differently because they're smarter. They manage them differently because they pay attention to different things."*

## Our Story (`about.html`)

- **Purpose:** Answer "why does this firm exist?" with a story, not a résumé.
- **One takeaway:** The discipline institutions receive every day rarely reaches families; Driftwood was built to change that.
- **Primary CTA:** Tax Lab ("see it on your own numbers"); secondary: How We Invest.
- **Supporting ideas:** The $400M realization · the founder (last, brief) · registered-and-public-about-it · the philosophy close.
- **Statistics allowed:** $400 million — and nothing else.
- **Statistics prohibited:** All performance and model figures. A story page with stats becomes a pitch.
- **Tone:** Personal, quiet, first-person allowed. The one page where the founder speaks as "I."
- **Words to avoid:** Any product name · adjectives of self-praise.
- **Related pages:** How We Invest (the beliefs) · The Driftwood Principles (the worldview).
- **Canonical quote:** *"Then something stopped making sense."* And the close: *"Markets will always be uncertain. Costs are not. Taxes are not. Discipline is not."*

## How We Invest (`thesis.html`)

- **Purpose:** Teach the investment philosophy — the process the beliefs produce.
- **One takeaway:** A handful of decisions matter more than everything else; Driftwood makes them deliberately instead of accidentally.
- **Primary CTA:** Tax Lab; secondary: Our Story.
- **Supporting ideas:** The six principles · how it comes together (one system, six coordinated parts) · the evidence lineage (Markowitz, Fama–French) · founder, last.
- **Statistics allowed:** None. Only enduring ideas. (The banner carries the positioning phrase, not a number.)
- **Statistics prohibited:** All performance discussion — the moment this page quotes a return, it stops being a philosophy.
- **Tone:** The narrator teaching how investing works; Driftwood appears as the one who behaves accordingly.
- **Words to avoid:** engine · product names except one quiet mention beneath the principles · any second use of "architecture" beyond the banner.
- **Related pages:** The Driftwood Principles (worldview vs process) · Research (the proof lives there, not here).
- **Canonical quote:** *"Our philosophy is simply an attempt to make those decisions deliberately instead of accidentally."*

## The Driftwood Principles (`principles.html`)

- **Purpose:** The evergreen essay — the firm's worldview, worth reading even with Driftwood's name removed. A bookmark, not a page.
- **One takeaway:** Return is not wealth; what compounds is what you keep and how you behave.
- **Primary CTA:** None by design. Two quiet links (How We Invest, Our Story) in the close. Essays don't sell.
- **Supporting ideas:** The five beliefs: return ≠ wealth · compounding hates interruptions · time is a family's edge · behavior is the multiplier · enough is a number.
- **Statistics allowed:** None, ever. Timelessness is the point.
- **Statistics prohibited:** All.
- **Tone:** Housel-adjacent plainness at Marks-memo altitude. Short paragraphs. One memorable sentence per section.
- **Words to avoid:** Product names · service language · anything that dates the essay.
- **Related pages:** Linked from Our Story and (eventually) Insights essays; deliberately out of the nav.
- **Canonical quote:** *"Calm is not a personality trait in investing. It is an asset."*

## Tax Lab (`taxlab.html`)

- **Purpose:** Answer "how would this apply to me?" — the personalization machine.
- **One takeaway:** Your after-tax picture is computable, and where each dollar sits changes it.
- **Primary CTA:** Book the 15-minute diagnostic (after the visitor sees their own number).
- **Supporting ideas:** Asset location · harvesting · estate coordination · the state map · sensitivity ranges.
- **Statistics allowed:** The visitor's own computed dollars (with visible assumptions and ± ranges) · bracket/state facts · model assumptions per the Evidence Register.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Ledger/backtest performance figures; the machine room quotes inputs and mechanics, not track records.
- **Tone:** Precise, functional, unhurried. A machine room should look like a machine room — clean, labeled, honest.
- **Words to avoid:** proprietary · optimization-as-magic · unlabeled point estimates.
- **Related pages:** Tax Diagnostic (the one-page version) · State Tax Guide.
- **Canonical quote:** The output itself — a dollar figure with its assumptions attached.

## Tax Diagnostic (`leakage.html`)

- **Purpose:** Answer "where am I losing money today?" on one page.
- **One takeaway:** The same holdings, taxed carelessly vs deliberately, end up in very different places — and the difference is measurable.
- **Primary CTA:** Run my analysis (Tax Lab).
- **Supporting ideas:** Before/after cards · the three levers · the state table · the honest inversion in the fine print.
- **Statistics allowed:** The recovery band (+3.7–4.7%/yr, the page's ONE number) · before/after keep bars · per-state after-tax growth · 9.4→9.1 pre-tax.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Model-portfolio returns, Sharpe, anything from the research layer.
- **Tone:** A leave-behind exhibit: one idea, demonstrated, dated, disclosed.
- **Words to avoid:** leakage-as-jargon in body copy (the mechanism is "losing return to tax") · product names above the table.
- **Related pages:** Tax Lab (deeper) · State Tax Guide (localize it).
- **Canonical quote:** *"Where the return leaks — and how we keep it."*

## State Tax Guide (`statemap.html` + 51 state pages)

- **Purpose:** Answer "what should I know if I live here?" — the utility layer.
- **One takeaway:** Where you live changes what you keep; here is your state, plainly.
- **Primary CTA:** The personalized diagnostic deep-link per state.
- **Statistics allowed:** State tax facts (rates, cliffs, QSBS, step-up) with the as-of-law framing · each state's illustrative recovery figure.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Performance figures of any kind.
- **Tone:** Reference-grade: factual, current, unadorned. Charm stays in the headline ("Fifty states, fifty tax personalities"), not the data.
- **Words to avoid:** Dated hooks (anniversaries, current events) · advice-flavored imperatives.
- **Related pages:** Tax Lab, Tax Diagnostic.
- **Canonical quote:** *"Fifty states, fifty tax personalities."*

## Single Asset Risk (`concentration.html`)

- **Purpose:** Teach the hardest problem in taxable wealth well enough that the reader trusts the teacher.
- **One takeaway:** There is no clean answer to concentrated stock — only tradeoffs, and they can be laid out calmly.
- **Primary CTA:** Talk it through (diagnostic).
- **Statistics allowed:** The 1–5 fit scores and 22×6 grid (labeled orientation) · committee fit notes.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Performance claims; dollar outcomes (that's the Tax Lab's job).
- **Tone:** Investment committee, not software. Progressive disclosure: calm table first, detail on demand.
- **Words to avoid:** dashboard · tool-as-identity · urgency.
- **Related pages:** Tax Lab (the dollar version of the same decision).
- **Canonical quote:** *"There is no clean answer, only tradeoffs."*

## Research layer (`equities.html` · `ledger.html` · `tearsheet.html` · `equities_case_studies.html`)

- **Purpose:** Demonstrate intellectual rigor for the reader who wants proof — and gatekeepers (CPAs, attorneys, sophisticated spouses).
- **One takeaway:** The work is real, dated, honest about being hypothetical, and was not fit to the past.
- **Primary CTA:** Back to the current book / the diagnostic; these pages convert by existing, not by selling.
- **Statistics allowed:** Everything, properly labeled: Sharpe, drawdown, attribution, backtests, regression, methodology, costs. This is where the numbers live.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Unlabeled or undated figures; the synthetic-control Sharpe outside its study; any figure not in the Evidence Register.
- **Tone:** Technical, precise, self-deprecating where honest (in-sample ≈ engineering validation, not expected returns).
- **Words to avoid:** "the proof" (retired) · engine · promises.
- **Related pages:** How We Invest (the philosophy these mechanics serve).
- **Canonical quote:** *"Built to persist, not to impress."*

## Insights (the publishing engine — governed by `Investment_Writing_Principles.md`)

- **Purpose:** Teach durable investment principles. The pages that make Driftwood a publication that
  happens to be an advisory firm. A permanent collection, not a blog.
- **The index (`insights.html`):** radically quiet — eyebrow, one dek, then a reverse-chronological list
  of **date + title only**. No thumbnails, excerpts, per-item deks, categories, reading times, bios, or
  social buttons. The restraint is the design.
- **One takeaway (per piece):** Exactly one; if it needs two, it's two pieces.
- **Primary CTA:** None inside a piece. A quiet firm note at the foot. Trust is the CTA.
- **Statistics allowed:** Sourced, evergreen facts (Evidence Register rules apply); no firm performance
  figures inside a piece.
- **Statistics prohibited:** Anything that turns a piece into a pitch; anything timely.
- **Publish because it's true, not because it's timely.** No market commentary, no news, no headline
  reactions, no SEO- or acquisition-driven writing. If it dates in twelve months, don't publish it.
- **Tone:** The Housel test — would this be worth reading with Driftwood's name removed?
- **Words to avoid:** "essay" (never in public copy — present only the title) · "Driftwood" as subject
  (the firm is the publisher, not the topic) · product names leading a piece.
- **Related pages:** pieces link to Principles and How We Invest where natural — never to product pages
  mid-piece; Research is the supporting evidence beneath them.
- **Canonical quote (flagship):** *"Every portfolio has two returns. The one it earns, and the one you
  actually keep."*
