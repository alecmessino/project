# The Driftwood Canon

*Not a backlog. A destination.*

The Idea Pipeline (`Idea_Pipeline.md`) is where ideas wait. The **Canon** is the small, permanent body
of work that, once written, will define Driftwood's thinking for a decade — the pieces a reader could hand
to a friend and say *"this is how to think about money."* Think of it the way a great fund thinks about
its shareholder letters, or a school about its founding texts: **twenty to thirty pieces, no more, each
one load-bearing.**

A pipeline title becomes a Canon piece only when it clears the bar in `Investment_Writing_Principles.md`
— above all, the test that matters most: **does it leave the reader with one memorable mental model they
can repeat?** Most good ideas won't make it. That is the point. Scarcity is what makes a canon a canon.

**Status:** ✅ published · ✍️ drafted (in reserve) · ○ commissioned (a Canon slot, not yet written)

## I · What you keep (taxes & after-tax wealth)
- ✅ **Every Portfolio Has Two Returns** — the one it earns, the one you keep *(the founding piece)*
- ○ The Invisible Expense Ratio — the cost that never appears on a statement
- ○ Why Taxes Compound — a dollar taxed early is every dollar it would have become
- ○ Every Portfolio Is Also an Estate Plan — you are always investing for two generations

## II · How the best investors think (institutional attention)
- ✍️ **The World's Largest Investors Pay Attention Differently** — access is overrated; attention isn't
- ○ The Attention Stack — allocation → location → taxes → estate → behavior → time
- ○ What an Endowment Actually Talks About — rarely which stock to buy

## III · Risk, diversification & certainty
- ○ The Diversification Illusion — owning many things is not diversification
- ○ Volatility Is a Price Tag, Not a Risk — the difference decides who stays invested
- ○ The Cost of Certainty — what you pay to feel safe

## IV · Behavior & the long game
- ✍️ **Enough Is a Number** — a portfolio funds a life, so "enough" can be calculated
- ○ The Best Portfolio Is One You Can Ignore — durability as a feature
- ○ The Cost of Selling Too Soon — the tax you pay for impatience
- ○ Decision Quality vs. Outcome Quality — judging the choice, not the year

## V · Compounding, cost & time
- ○ Wealth Dies in Friction — small leaks, long voyages
- ○ Time Is an Asset Class — the family's one structural advantage
- ○ Patience Has a Yield — what waiting is actually worth

## VI · First principles
- ○ Evidence Over Prediction — why we don't forecast
- ○ The Quiet Mathematics of Wealth — the arithmetic nobody markets
- ○ Two Investors, Same Fund, Different Fortunes — behavior is the multiplier

---

**How the Canon grows.** One piece at a time, deliberately, from the pipeline. When a commissioned slot is
filled, its mental model is added to the Driftwood Lexicon. When the Canon reaches roughly two dozen
pieces, it becomes the spine of the first annual **Driftwood Letter** — and the website stops being the
product and becomes the library.
