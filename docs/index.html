Our thesis

The largest investors in the world don't manage portfolios differently because they're smarter. They manage them differently because they pay attention to different things.

Taxes. Asset location. Rebalancing. Diversification. Costs. Implementation. None of those are features — they're the things that decide what you keep. That attention — coordinated across everyone who touches your wealth — is what Driftwood brings to individual investors.

One household, coordinated

Most families' wealth is run in pieces — the portfolio in one place, the tax return in another, the estate plan in a third. We work the way a family office does: the seat at the table that keeps every piece coordinated, and methodical, as one after-tax plan.

How it works

Our objective isn't the highest pre-tax return. It's helping you keep more after taxes — in the model, 9.1% pre-tax produced more wealth than 9.4%, because less was lost to taxes along the way.

The case for keeping more

On $1 million of realized gains, the difference between a naive and a coordinated approach compounds. Over 30 years, an illustrative $410k stays working for you — against $90k when the same gains are handed to taxes as they go.
