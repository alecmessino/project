About Driftwood

Wealth is what you keep.

Every portfolio is measured before taxes. Every family spends after them. Driftwood exists to close that gap.

Why we exist

The largest cost most investors ever pay isn't a fee. It's tax.

It's paid slowly, quietly, over decades — on gains few investors ever chose to realize. Most management optimizes the return and treats the tax bill as something that happens afterward. Driftwood reverses the order. We decide where each dollar belongs before we decide what it holds, because the account a position lives in often matters more than the position itself.

That is the whole idea, and it is enough to build a firm around: manage the after-tax result as deliberately as most firms manage the pre-tax return.

Who Driftwood is for

Coordination is worth more than any single product.

We are built for people whose finances are complex enough that the pieces have to work together — where the tax return, the estate plan, and the portfolio are one problem, not three.

Business owners & founders
Concentrated wealth, uneven income, and a liquidity event to plan around.
Executives with equity
RSUs, options, and vesting that arrive faster than a plan for them does.
Concentrated-stock households
A single large, low-basis position that is hard to sell and risky to keep.
High-tax families
Households where state and federal rates make every realized dollar expensive.
Clients who work with a CPA or attorney
People who want their advisers coordinated — not talking past each other.
How we work

A fiduciary is simply someone required to put your interests first.

We built the firm so that is the only way it can operate. Driftwood is fee-only — we are paid by our clients and by no one else. There are no commissions, no products sold to you, and no incentive that points anywhere but at your outcome.

✓ Fee-only, fiduciary ✓ No commissions or product sales ✓ Coordinated with your CPA & attorney

We work with your existing advisers rather than around them. The result is one after-tax plan — not a pile of accounts that each look reasonable on their own and add up to a tax bill no one was watching.

Our record, in the open

We show our work — and we don't revise it.

Our model portfolio is append-only: every rebalance is dated and permanent, so the record you see is the record as it happened, not a version tidied up afterward. When our thinking changes, the change is dated too. We would rather be judged by a history that cannot be quietly edited than by a number chosen in hindsight.

The full research — the model, its evidence, and how the approach has evolved — is open to read in our Approach and Research.
