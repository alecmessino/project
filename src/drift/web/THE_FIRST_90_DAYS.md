# The First 90 Days — Driftwood Onboarding Operating Procedure

*A runbook, not a brochure. This designs the first client experience so that it produces the
governance system defined in [GOVERNANCE_ARCHITECTURE.md](GOVERNANCE_ARCHITECTURE.md) (v1.1) as a
natural by-product. The architecture is a fixed specification here; this document works one layer
below it. Aligned to Governance Architecture v1.1 — the Decision Record now carries Dependencies and
an owned/cadenced Trigger, and the registers are views over the Decision Log.*

**The milestone this serves.** The next milestone is not a screen. It is proving that a real
household can complete this process using only a **PDF worksheet, a few working documents, and three
meetings.** If they can, the operating system is real and software is leverage on a proven workflow.
If they can't, more screens will only hide what's missing. Everything below is therefore designed to
run **on paper first.** No software is required to publish a Manual v1.

**The one-line problem.** Take a brand-new household from *"we have documents"* to *"we have
institutional judgment preserved" —* and do it so the records still make sense to a different advisor
ten years from now.

## The 90-day arc

Three client meetings, four working artifacts, one published Manual. Everything an advisor writes is
a **Decision Record** or a **read over Decision Records** — never a parallel source of truth.

| Phase | Weeks | Client meeting | What gets produced | Records touched |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **0 · Engage & Prime** | 0–1 | — | Engagement letter; worksheet issued | — |
| **1 · Intent & Inventory** | 2–3 | **M1 · The Intent Conversation** (90m) | Intent Brief; Structure Inventory | Records *stubbed*, not yet written |
| **2 · Retroactive Judgment Capture** | 3–5 | **M2 · The Provenance Session** (90m) | The first batch of Decision Records | ∅ → **Open** |
| **3 · Synthesis & Charter** | 6–9 | **M3 · The Charter Session** (2h) | Wealth Operating Manual v1 (draft) | Open → **Confirmed**; forward decisions opened |
| **4 · Publish & Cadence** | 10–12 | — | Manual v1 published; calendar & trigger-watch | Cadence established |

The **Charter Session (M3) is by definition the first Operating Session** — it does exactly what
every future session does (confirm, open, supersede, set triggers), and it happens to be the one that
ratifies the founding Manual.

---

## Phase 0 · Engage & Prime (Week 0–1)

**Purpose:** set the relationship as ongoing *governance*, not a one-time review, and get the raw
material moving before the first meeting.

**Advisor does**
1. Issue the **engagement letter** framed as a coordinating governance mandate. (Language note: this
   is governance — the ongoing product — not an "audit." The word *audit* is reserved for the
   annual re-review of *other* advisors, never for Driftwood's own architecture.)
2. Send the client **Instrument A · The Intake & Intent Worksheet** with a document-request checklist.

**Client does**
- Gathers documents: trust instruments, entity/formation docs and operating agreements, the last two
  filed returns, account statements, insurance policies, the will and any estate documents, and
  beneficiary designations.
- Completes **Part 2 (Intent)** of the worksheet in their own words. Part 1 (Facts) can be partial;
  Part 2 is the one that matters and is done *before* M1.

**Definition of done:** documents received; Intent portion of the worksheet returned.

## Phase 1 · Intent & Inventory (Week 2–3)

### Meeting 1 — The Intent Conversation (90 min)

**This is not a data-collection meeting.** Facts come from documents. This meeting captures the
thing documents never contain: *what the family is trying to accomplish, what they fear, and the
language they use for their own life.* That intent is the yardstick every future decision is measured
against, so it must be captured in the client's words, not paraphrased into advice.

Run the **two-question domain pass** (Instrument C) lightly here at the level of the whole household:
- *"When you picture this having gone right in twenty years, what is true?"*
- *"What must never happen?"*
- Per domain, briefly: *"What is each major structure supposed to accomplish?"* and *"What would make
  you reconsider it?"* — flagging, not yet resolving.

**Output — the Intent Brief:** one page, in the client's language, stating goals, non-negotiables,
and fears. It is an input to every Decision Record's Rationale field. It is not itself a record.

### Between M1 and M2 — build the Structure Inventory

The advisor catalogs **every existing structure** from the documents: each trust, each entity, each
account-titling choice, each policy, each beneficiary designation, each estate instrument. One line
each. Every line will become either a Decision Record (its rationale is recoverable) or a Placeholder
(rationale pending). Nothing is re-described in prose; everything gets provenance.

**Definition of done:** Intent Brief written; Structure Inventory complete (every structure has a
line and a proposed disposition: *capture* or *placeholder*).

---

## Phase 2 · Retroactive Judgment Capture (Week 3–5)

This is the hard, distinctive phase — the one that converts "we have documents" into "we have
judgment preserved." The rationale behind existing structures usually lives in the head of the
attorney or CPA who built them, or is lost. The Provenance Session recovers it, or honestly marks
where it can't be recovered yet.

### Meeting 2 — The Provenance Session (90 min)

Go **structure by structure** down the Inventory. For each, ask the three questions that make a
record durable:
1. **What was this meant to accomplish?** (Rationale)
2. **What did it assume — about the law, the money, the family — at the time?** (Assumptions)
3. **What would make us reconsider it?** (Trigger)

Then resolve each line by the **provenance ladder**:

| The client… | Disposition | Record written as |
|---|---|---|
| knows the rationale | **Capture** | Open record, rationale in their words |
| half-remembers it | **Capture + verify** | Open record; assumptions flagged "to verify with [professional]" |
| doesn't know (a professional set it up) | **Reconstruct** | Open record marked *"rationale reconstructed — to confirm with [professional]"*; Driftwood proposes the likely reasoning from the structure + facts, never asserting false certainty |
| can't be resolved this cycle | **Placeholder** | Open (placeholder); owner + due date assigned; surfaces as *Next session* in the Manual |

**Reconstruction is itself a judgment act.** Proposing "this ILIT was almost certainly built to keep
a $2M death benefit out of a taxable estate and to fund the estate-tax bill without a forced sale —
does that match your understanding?" captures judgment even when memory has failed, provided it is
clearly labeled as reconstructed-and-to-confirm.

**A second output falls out of this session for free:** wherever a structure's original rationale no
longer holds under today's facts, that is a **finding** — it becomes a line in the Manual's
Opportunity or Risk register, each traced back to the record that exposed it.

### Between M2 and M3 — draft the records; close the gaps

- Draft each Decision Record from the session (Instrument B), one per structure.
- **Coordinate with the client's CPA/attorney** to fill "to verify" and "to confirm" items — this is
  the coordinating-principal role in practice, directing the bench rather than re-doing their work.
- Every record enters the **Decision Log** (the single source of truth) in **Open** state.

**Definition of done:** every Inventory line resolved to an Open record or an owned Placeholder;
verification requests out to the relevant professionals; findings noted for the registers.

## Phase 3 · Synthesis & the Charter Session (Week 6–9)

### Between M2 and M3 — assemble the Manual as a *read*

The Wealth Operating Manual v1 is **assembled entirely as a view over the Decision Log** — it invents
nothing. Each section is a projection of records:

| Manual section | Reads from |
|---|---|
| Team & who owns what | Record owners |
| Balance sheet & ownership | Structures, each with a Basis → its record (or *Next session*) |
| Coordination Index | The household's structural drivers (a qualifier, not a fee) |
| Operating calendar | The triggers and review dates across all records |
| Decision dashboard (forward) | The forward decisions opened for the next 12–36 months |
| Opportunity & Risk registers | **Filtered views over the Decision Log** (v1.1) — never a parallel list. Each line *is* a record: an opportunity/risk noticed but not yet decided is an Open record in *Identified* status. |

Before M3, the advisor also **opens the forward decisions** — the next 12–36 months of choices (a
sale, a move, a conversion ladder) — each as an Open record with a rationale, assumptions, and a
trigger, so the future is governed the same way the past now is.

### Meeting 3 — The Charter Session (2 hours) — *the first Operating Session*

Walk the Manual end to end. This meeting performs the standard Operating Session transitions on the
founding set of records:
- **Confirm** each retroactive record whose rationale is captured and whose assumptions the client
  (and, where needed, their professional) validate: **Open → Confirmed.**
- **Open** the forward decisions and **set their triggers** explicitly, in the client's words.
- **Supersede** on the spot any structure the session concludes no longer serves — the old record is
  marked Superseded and a successor is opened (never edited).
- **Assign owners and due dates** to every remaining Placeholder.

**Output:** a ratified Manual v1 (draft), a Decision Log with a real Open/Confirmed population, and an
agreed trigger-watch list.

**Definition of done:** Manual walked and ratified; founding records confirmed; forward decisions
opened; placeholders owned.

---

## Phase 4 · Publish & Set the Cadence (Week 10–12)

**Advisor does**
1. **Publish Manual v1** — dated and versioned (`v1`, with a stamped issue date). It is a point-in-time
   authored document, not a live feed.
2. Stand up the **operating calendar** (the annual rhythm) and the **trigger-watch list** (the union
   of every open record's trigger — the things that, if they happen, reopen a decision). Per v1.1,
   **each trigger names its owner and cadence** — Driftwood-continuous, periodic, or client-surfaced —
   so the list is an enforceable control, not a wish; client-surfaced triggers are the ones the AWOR
   must actively *ask* about.
3. Schedule the cadence: quarterly touchpoints and the annual **AWOR**, whose job is to re-read every
   open record and ask *did its assumption hold, did its trigger fire?* — a review measured against
   recorded intent, never a recap of the year.

**Definition of done:** Manual v1 published; cadence and trigger-watch established; the household now
runs on a Decision Log.

## Definition of Done — the 90-day exit criteria

The engagement has produced the operating system when all of the following are true:

1. **Structure Inventory is exhaustive** — every pre-existing structure is catalogued.
2. **Every inventory line is resolved** to one of: a **Confirmed** record, an **Open** record
   (rationale captured, verification pending), or an **owned Placeholder** (owner + due date).
3. **Forward decisions are governed** — each 12–36-month decision is an Open record with a trigger.
4. **Manual v1 is published** — dated, versioned, and every line item carries a Basis (a record or an
   honest *Next session*).
5. **The first Operating Session (Charter) has occurred** and the cadence + trigger-watch are set.
6. **The Decision Log is the single source of truth** — the Manual and everything else read from it;
   no parallel record of decisions exists anywhere.

**The proof test.** If steps 1–6 were achieved with only the worksheet, the record forms, working
documents, and three meetings — **no software** — then the operating system is proven. Software's job
from that point is to remove friction from a workflow that already works, never to supply a step the
paper process couldn't.

## The Instruments (print-ready)

Four instruments run the whole procedure. Each is designed to be lifted out as its own PDF or Word
document.

### Instrument A · The Intake & Intent Worksheet *(client-facing)*

**Part 1 — Facts** (may be partial before M1): household & family; the balance sheet (asset, how
held, rough value); entities and trusts (name, type, year formed); accounts (type, owner);
insurance; a document checklist.

**Part 2 — Intent** (required before M1; this is the differentiating half). Open questions, answered
in the client's own words:
- When you picture your financial life having gone *right* in twenty years, what is true?
- What must **never** happen?
- Who are you responsible for — now and eventually?
- What is the hardest financial decision ahead of you in the next few years?
- For each major structure you already have (a trust, an LLC, a policy): what do you believe it was
  set up to do — and do you actually know, or are you guessing?
- What has an advisor done for you that you never understood the reason for?

> The last two questions are the ones that make the Provenance Session productive. They tell you, in
> advance, where rationale is known, guessed, or lost.

### Instrument B · The Decision Record *(the atomic form — one per structure/decision)*

A one-page fill-in form. The three starred fields are non-negotiable; a form missing any of them is
not yet a record. (v1.1 added **Dependencies**, and an **owner + cadence** on the Trigger.)

```
DECISION RECORD  ·  DR-____                         State: ○ Open  ○ Confirmed  ○ Superseded  ○ Archived
Household: __________________________   Domain: ______________   Date decided: __________
Owner (accountable name): _______________________________________________________________

WHAT WAS DECIDED
  ______________________________________________________________________________________

★ RATIONALE  (why — in the household's own goals, not generic advice)
  ______________________________________________________________________________________
  ______________________________________________________________________________________

  ASSUMPTIONS AT THE TIME  (law · rates · family facts)          ☐ reconstructed — to confirm with ______
  1) __________________________  2) __________________________  3) __________________________

  ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED  (what was rejected, and why)
  ______________________________________________________________________________________

★ TRIGGER  (the condition that would reopen this decision)
  ______________________________________________________________________________________
     Watched by: ○ Driftwood  ○ Client  ○ ______________     Cadence: ○ continuous  ○ periodic ____  ○ client-surfaced

  DEPENDENCIES   Depends on: DR-____, DR-____        Blocks: DR-____, DR-____   (execution order — keep acyclic)

★ LINEAGE   Supersedes: DR-____        Superseded by: DR-____        Last confirmed: __________
```

### Instrument C · The Provenance Interview Guide *(advisor-facing)*

For each domain, the two-question intent pass plus a reconstruction prompt. Domains: **Estate ·
Tax · Entities/Business · Investments/Concentration · Insurance · Liquidity · Family/Next-generation.**

Per domain:
1. *What is each structure here meant to accomplish?* → Rationale
2. *What would make you reconsider it?* → Trigger
3. If unknown: *Here is what a structure like this is usually for, given your facts — does that match?*
   → reconstructed rationale, marked to-confirm.

Resolve every line by the **provenance ladder** (Capture / Capture+verify / Reconstruct / Placeholder)
from Phase 2.

### Instrument D · The Operating Session Agenda *(recurring — used at the Charter Session and every one after)*

1. **Trigger check** — for each open/confirmed record: did its assumption hold? did its trigger fire?
2. **Confirm** the records that still hold (date them).
3. **Supersede** the records whose trigger fired — open a successor; never edit the original.
4. **Open** any new decisions surfaced since last session.
5. **Resolve placeholders** due this session.
6. **Re-issue** the Manual if anything changed, with a stamped "what changed."

## A worked example — a durable retroactive record

What "still makes sense to a different advisor in ten years" looks like, produced from the Provenance
Session for a structure that predated the engagement:

```
DECISION RECORD · DR-001                                                     State: ● Confirmed
Household: Harris        Domain: Estate        Date decided: (structure predates engagement; captured Yr 0)
Owner: Estate attorney + Driftwood

WHAT WAS DECIDED
  The $2M life policy is owned by an irrevocable life insurance trust (ILIT), not personally.

★ RATIONALE
  Held personally, the death benefit falls inside an already-$8M estate and worsens an estate-tax
  exposure the family's liquid assets can't cover in the nine-month window. The ILIT removes it from
  the estate AND creates liquidity that arrives exactly when the estate-tax bill is due — so the
  business and the concentrated stock are not force-sold under a clock.

  ASSUMPTIONS AT THE TIME                                       ☐ reconstructed — to confirm: n/a (client-confirmed)
  1) Federal estate exemption & portability as currently in effect
  2) Policy remains in force; Tom remains the insured
  3) Estate continues to exceed the exemption as it grows

  ALTERNATIVES CONSIDERED
  Leave it personally owned and fund the estate-tax bill from a future business sale — rejected; a
  forced sale on a nine-month clock destroys value.

★ TRIGGER
  Reopen if the federal exemption is materially cut, the benefit no longer covers projected estate
  tax as the estate grows, or Tom's insurability changes.
     Watched by: Driftwood (exemption law · coverage ratio) + Client (insurability)   Cadence: continuous + annual AWOR

  DEPENDENCIES   Depends on: —        Blocks: — (the estate-liquidity plan reads from this record)

★ LINEAGE   Supersedes: —     Superseded by: —     Last confirmed: Yr 0 Charter Session
```

The *decision* ("use an ILIT") is ordinary and will someday be obsolete. The *judgment* — the
reasoning, the assumptions it rests on, and the exact conditions that would reopen it — is what a
future advisor inherits instead of reconstructing from scratch. That inheritance is the product.
